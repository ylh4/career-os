# Owning an ambiguous cost mandate

- **Themes:** ambiguity, ownership, influence without authority, results
- **Situation:** Finance flagged runaway cloud spend (2024–25) but there was no owner, no
  attribution, and no mandate — just "someone should fix this."
- **Task:** Turn a vague problem into a tractable program and actually move the number,
  across teams I didn't manage.
- **Action:** I started by making the problem visible — built per-service cost attribution
  dashboards so each team could see their own spend. Then I chaired a monthly efficiency
  review, picked high-leverage targets (right-sizing, spot for batch, autoscaling SLOs),
  and let teams own their cuts rather than mandating them.
- **Result:** **$1.8M/yr (-23%)** reduction with no SLA regressions; cost-per-order down 31%;
  the dashboards are now the standard governance tool.
- **Source:** Ties to [cost-savings]; finance savings report FY25.
- **Reflection:** With ambiguous, cross-team work, visibility plus a recurring forum beat
  top-down mandates — people fix what they can see and own.
